STUDY ID: PL-2024-UNSUP

Análisis de Perfiles de Personalidad

Unsupervised Learning approach to identifying latent behavioral patterns. By applying advanced clustering techniques to psychometric data, we categorize individuals into four distinct typologies: Extrovertido, Sociable, Reservado, and Introvertido.

MOTIVATION

Our goal is to identify natural personality patterns without prior labels, grouping users by psychological traits to enable highly personalized digital experiences and targeted interactions based on inherent behavioral characteristics.

DATA.MODEL.SUMMARY
Variables (N) 7
Target Clusters (k) 4
Primary Algorithm K-Means
Dataset Size 1,834 records

State of the Art

ALGORITHMS EXPLAINED

scatter_plot

K-Means

A centroid-based clustering algorithm that partitions data into distinct groups based on proximity to the cluster center. Highly efficient for structured, spherical data distributions.

bubble_chart

DBSCAN

Density-based spatial clustering of applications with noise. Groups together closely packed points, making it excellent for identifying irregular shapes and filtering out noise or outliers.

account_tree

Hierarchical Clustering

Agglomerative clustering that builds a hierarchy or "family relations" between data points. Useful for understanding the structural relationship of the data through dendrograms without pre-specifying cluster counts.

compress

PCA (Principal Component Analysis)

A dimensionality reduction technique used here to simplify 7 complex behavioral variables down to 2 principal axes (components), preserving variance while enabling 2D visualization and interpretation.

Technical Flow

1

Normalization

Applied StandardScaler to ensure all variables contribute equally to the distance calculations, preventing features with larger scales from dominating the model.

2

The Elbow Method

Plotted Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS) to identify the optimal number of clusters. The inflection point clearly indicated k=4 as the target.

3

K-Means Execution

Executed the K-Means algorithm with k=4 on the scaled data to assign each user record to a specific personality archetype.

4

Validation via DBSCAN & Hierarchical

Cross-validated the K-Means results by running Hierarchical clustering (confirming structural groups) and checking DBSCAN to understand density and potential noise.

Conclusions & Recommendations

The unsupervised learning segmentation successfully distilled the dataset of 1,834 records into four actionable persona profiles. PCA played a crucial role in enabling human interpretation of these complex multidimensional relationships. We observed strong consistency across the different methodologies applied.

RECOMMENDATIONS

  • Implement Silhouette Coefficient for rigorous mathematical validation of cluster cohesion and separation metrics.
  • Introduce a pre-processing step using DBSCAN specifically targeted at data cleaning to remove noise before running centroid-based models.
